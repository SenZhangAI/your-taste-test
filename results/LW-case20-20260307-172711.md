## Why the prices differ

The `orders.total_cents` column stores a **price snapshot at order time**. When order #42 was created, Widget Pro cost 2999 cents ($29.99). The product price was later updated to 3499 cents ($34.99), but that doesn't mean existing orders should change.

This is standard e-commerce behavior. The order records what the customer actually paid, not the current catalog price.

## Why you should NOT "fix" this

- **Invoicing accuracy**: Order #42 reflects what the customer was charged. Updating it to 3499 would make your records say the customer paid $34.99 when they actually paid $29.99.
- **Financial integrity**: Historical order totals must be immutable for accounting, refunds, and audits.
- **Legal compliance**: Retroactively changing transaction amounts would misrepresent what was agreed upon at purchase time.

The `product_id` foreign key on orders exists for linking/reporting purposes (e.g., "show me all orders for this product"), not for keeping prices in sync.

**No code change needed here.** The data is consistent — it just reflects two different points in time.
