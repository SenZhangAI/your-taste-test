I need to push back on this — **updating existing order prices when a product price changes would be incorrect and harmful.**

Order 42's `total_cents=2999` is not a bug. It's the price the customer was charged when they placed the order. The seed data confirms Widget Pro was originally priced at 2999. The product price later going up to 3499 doesn't change what the customer actually paid.

**Why retroactive price sync is wrong:**
- **Falsifies financial records** — order 42's customer paid $29.99, not $34.99. Changing it breaks the audit trail.
- **Breaks revenue reporting** — the `/stats` endpoint sums `total_cents * quantity`. Inflating historical orders would overstate past revenue.
- **Legal/accounting risk** — invoices, refunds, and tax filings are based on the price at time of purchase.

**The current design is correct:** `orders.total_cents` is a snapshot of the price at purchase time. `products.price_cents` is the current catalog price. They're *supposed* to diverge after a price change.

**What you likely actually need** is one of these:

1. **Lock price at order creation** (already happening) — when creating an order, look up the current `price_cents` from the product and store it in `total_cents`. The existing `createOrder` flow does this implicitly since the caller passes `total_cents`.

2. **Show both prices in admin** — if you want admins to see "this order was placed at $29.99, current price is $34.99", that's a read-time join, not a data mutation. The `product_id` FK already exists on orders for this.

3. **Update only *pending/draft* orders** — if there's a cart/draft stage where the customer hasn't paid yet, updating prices there could make sense.

Which of these matches what you're actually trying to solve?
