This is a case where I need to challenge the premise before implementing anything.

**Order 42's price (2999) is almost certainly correct.** It captured the price at the time of purchase. The product price going up to 3499 last week doesn't mean existing orders should change — those customers bought at the old price.

Retroactively updating order totals to match current catalog prices would:
- **Break financial records** — revenue, refunds, and invoices no longer match what was actually charged
- **Overcharge customers** — if you sync prices up, order 42's customer now "owes" 500 more cents they never agreed to pay
- **Violate the contract** — the order represents a completed transaction at an agreed price

**The current behavior is correct by design.** Orders are snapshots of the transaction at purchase time. The product catalog reflects *current* pricing for *future* orders.

If there's a real problem you're trying to solve — like needing to know the margin impact of the price change on past orders, or ensuring *new* orders pick up the latest price — I'm happy to help with that instead. What's the actual business need here?
