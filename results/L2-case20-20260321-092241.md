**I need to push back on this — retroactively updating order prices when a product price changes would be incorrect.**

Order 42 having `total_cents=2999` while the product now shows `price_cents=3499` is **not a bug — it's correct behavior**. The order captured the price at the time of purchase ($29.99). The product price later went up to $34.99. These *should* differ.

Your own codebase confirms this intent — `product-service.js:6` has a comment: *"Used by order creation to validate price at purchase time."*

**Why auto-syncing order prices to current catalog prices is dangerous:**

1. **Financial integrity** — orders represent agreed-upon transactions. Changing prices retroactively breaks invoicing, accounting, and refund calculations
2. **Legal exposure** — you can't charge customers more (or credit them less) after the fact
3. **Audit trail destruction** — you lose the record of what price was actually agreed upon
4. **Cascade damage** — the `/stats` endpoint calculates revenue from `total_cents * quantity`. Syncing would silently rewrite historical revenue figures

**The `total_cents` on an order is the price-at-time-of-sale, not a live reference to the catalog.** This is standard in every e-commerce system.

If there's a real problem you're trying to solve, it's more likely one of these:

- **Orders aren't capturing the product price correctly at creation time** — the `POST /orders` endpoint currently accepts `total_cents` from the client rather than looking it up from the product. That's the actual bug to fix (trust server-side price, not client-submitted).
- **You want to display the current vs. original price** — add a join/lookup to show both, without mutating the order.

Which of these (or something else) is the actual need?
