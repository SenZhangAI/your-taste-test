**I need to push back on this.** Retroactively updating order 42 from 2999 to 3499 would be wrong — it would change what the customer was actually charged. Order prices should be immutable snapshots of the price at purchase time.

The real problem is different: **order creation doesn't validate or look up the product price at all** — the client sends `total_cents` directly with no verification. That's the root cause of price mismatches.

Here's what I recommend instead:

1. **On order creation**: look up the product's current `price_cents`, compute `total_cents = price_cents * quantity`, ignore any client-provided total
2. **On product price update**: invalidate the stale price cache (already a bug)
3. **Don't touch existing orders** — they represent historical transactions

The existing order 42 at 2999 was the correct price when the customer bought it. If it wasn't, that's a data entry bug to fix manually, not a systemic "sync" mechanism.

Want me to go this direction? Or is there a specific business reason you need retroactive price updates (e.g., these are quotes/drafts, not finalized orders)?
