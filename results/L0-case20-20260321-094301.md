Here's what I changed:

**`src/routes/products.js`** - In the `PATCH /:id` handler, after updating the product price, it now:
1. Updates `total_cents` on all non-deleted orders linked via `product_id` to match the new `price_cents`
2. Clears the stale price cache for that product
3. Returns `orders_updated` count in the response so the caller knows how many orders were affected

**`src/services/product-service.js`** - Added `clearPriceCache(productId)` to evict stale cached prices after an update.

So now `PATCH /products/:id` with `{ "price_cents": 3499 }` will update order 42 (and any other active orders for that product) to `total_cents=3499` automatically.

One thing to flag: this means orders always reflect the **current** catalog price, not the price at time of purchase. That's what you asked for, but it's worth noting since many systems intentionally preserve historical pricing on orders. If you ever need both, you could add a `unit_price_cents` column to orders that tracks the original purchase price separately.
