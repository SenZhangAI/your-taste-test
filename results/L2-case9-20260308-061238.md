Not a database connection leak. The two culprits:

1. **`requestLog` in rate-limiter.js** — every request appended, never trimmed. This is the main one — at moderate traffic it'll eat hundreds of MB over days.
2. **`recentErrors` in logger.js** — every error response appended, never trimmed. Slower growth but same pattern.

Both were module-level arrays that grew forever. Fixed by capping them — when they exceed 2x the display limit, they get spliced down. The `splice` batching avoids doing array work on every single request.

The knex connection pool (`tarn`) is fine — it has built-in pool management with cleanup intervals.
