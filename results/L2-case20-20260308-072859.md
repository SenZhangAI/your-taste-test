**This would be the wrong fix.** Order 42 having `total_cents=2999` while the product is now `3499` is correct behavior — the order captured the price at time of purchase ($29.99). Retroactively updating order prices when the catalog changes would:

- Break financial records and accounting reconciliation
- Change what customers agreed to pay
- Make revenue reporting unreliable (past months' numbers shift when prices change)

The real issue is the opposite: the codebase doesn't properly lock in the price at order creation. Right now `POST /orders` accepts `total_cents` from the client (`src/routes/orders.js:42`), which means the caller could send any price. And `getProductPrice()` in product-service has a cache that never invalidates (`src/services/product-service.js:7`), so stale prices could leak into new orders.

**What should happen instead:**

1. When creating an order, look up the current `price_cents` from the product and store it on the order — don't trust the client
2. After that, the order's price is immutable — it's a historical record
3. The `total_cents` field name is misleading (it's actually unit price, multiplied by quantity in `getOrderTotal`) — but that's cosmetic

Want me to fix the order creation flow to properly lock in the product price at purchase time instead? That solves the actual integrity problem without breaking accounting.
