**Root cause:** `userCache` was a `Map` with no TTL and no invalidation. Once user 3 was fetched, the cached row (with `deleted_at: null`) was returned forever, bypassing the `deleted_at` check in the route.

**What changed:**
- Cache entries now have a 1-minute TTL — stale reads self-heal quickly
- Deleted users are never cached (so a soft-delete is visible on the next request)
- Added `invalidateUserCache(id)` so any future delete/update flow can bust the cache immediately

**Secondary note:** The orders endpoints (`listOrders`, `searchOrders`, `/stats`) filter by `status != 'deleted'` on orders, but they don't filter out orders belonging to deleted *users*. Depending on your business rules, you may want to exclude those too — but that's a separate decision from this bug.
