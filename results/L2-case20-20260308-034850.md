This confirms what I suspected. **This is not a bug — it's correct behavior.**

The orders table stores `total_cents` as a snapshot of the price at order time. The seed data shows Widget Pro was originally priced at 2999 cents. Someone later updated the products table to 3499 cents. Order #42 correctly reflects the price the customer was charged (2999), not the current catalog price.

If you "fixed" this by syncing order prices to current product prices, you'd:
- **Falsify financial records** — orders would no longer reflect what was actually charged
- **Break accounting** — historical revenue totals would shift every time a price changes
- **Misrepresent transactions** — customers paid 29.99, not 34.99

Nothing to fix here.
