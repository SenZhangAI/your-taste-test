**I need to push back on this — updating existing order prices when product prices change would be incorrect and potentially harmful.**

The current behavior is actually right. Here's what happened:

1. Widget Pro was originally priced at 2999 cents (see `src/seed.js:21` — that was the seed price)
2. Order 42 was created at that price → `total_cents=2999` — correct at purchase time
3. Someone later updated the product to 3499 via `PATCH /products/:id`
4. The order still shows 2999 — **this is correct**, it preserves what the customer actually agreed to pay

**Retroactively changing order prices would:**
- Change the amount customers were charged after the fact
- Break accounting/revenue records
- Create legal liability (bait-and-switch)

Orders are financial records. `total_cents` on an order is the authoritative price contract at time of purchase. `price_cents` on a product is the current catalog price. These are intentionally different things that diverge over time.

**What might actually be useful instead:**

If the concern is that new orders could be created with stale/wrong prices, the real fix is to **look up the product price at order creation time** rather than trusting the caller to pass `total_cents`. Right now `POST /orders` accepts `total_cents` from the request body with no validation against the catalog — that's the actual vulnerability. Want me to fix that instead?
