This is actually **not a bug** — it's working as designed. You should **not** add sync logic here.

The `total_cents` on an order is a **price snapshot** captured at the time the order was placed. The API docs confirm this at line 32: *"Price is captured at order time."* Order 42 having `total_cents=2999` while the product now shows `price_cents=3499` simply means the customer bought Widget Pro at $29.99, and the price was later raised to $34.99.

This is standard e-commerce behavior. If you retroactively updated order prices to match the current catalog:

- **Invoices and receipts would be wrong** — the customer was charged $29.99, not $34.99
- **Revenue reporting would break** — historical totals would shift every time a price changes
- **Refund calculations would be incorrect** — you'd refund the wrong amount
- **Legal/audit issues** — order records should reflect what the customer actually paid

The `product_id` FK on orders (added in migration 003) exists for stock management and linking orders back to products — not for keeping prices in sync. And the `getProductPrice()` function with its cache in `product-service.js` is used during order *creation* to look up the current price at purchase time, not to update existing orders afterward.

In short: the data is consistent. Each order correctly records what the customer paid at the time of purchase.
