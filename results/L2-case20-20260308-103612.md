This is correct behavior, not a bug. Order 42's total_cents=2999 reflects the price **at the time of purchase**. That's the price the customer agreed to and was charged.

**Retroactively updating order prices when products change would:**
- Change what customers were billed after the fact (legal/financial issue)
- Break accounting records and revenue reports
- Make refund calculations wrong (refund at new price vs. what was paid)
- Create audit trail nightmares

Orders are a **point-in-time snapshot** of the transaction. The product catalog is the *current* price. These are intentionally decoupled — every e-commerce system works this way.

**What you likely want instead** depends on the actual need:

- **"New orders should use the new price"** → Already happening if orders read from the products table at creation time. Verify that order creation captures `price_cents` from the product at checkout.
- **"I need to see which orders used an old price"** → Query `orders WHERE product_id = X AND total_cents != (SELECT price_cents FROM products WHERE id = X)`.
- **"Price history tracking"** → Add a `product_price_history` table that logs price changes with timestamps.
