Here's the summary of what was wrong and what I fixed:

**Root cause — not a missing index.** The `/stats` endpoint was fetching **all 500k order rows into Node.js memory** and then computing totals with `.length`, `.reduce()`, and `.forEach()`. That's why it takes 30 seconds in prod but works fine in dev with small test data.

**Fixes applied:**

1. **Replaced JS aggregation with SQL aggregation** (`src/routes/orders.js`):
   - `COUNT(*)` instead of `orders.length`
   - `SUM(total_cents * quantity)` instead of `.reduce()`
   - `GROUP BY status` with `COUNT(*)` instead of `.forEach()`
   
   This means the database does the math and returns a few small rows instead of 500k rows.

2. **Fixed route ordering** — `/stats` and `/search` were defined *after* `/:id`, so Express would match `GET /orders/stats` as `/:id` with `id="stats"`. Moved `/:id` after the named routes.

3. **Added an index on `status`** (`migrations/004-add-status-index.js`) — this helps all queries filtering by `WHERE status != 'deleted'`, including the list page's count query, but it's a secondary optimization, not the root fix.
