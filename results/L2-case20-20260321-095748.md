## Why the "mismatch" is correct behavior

Order 42 has `total_cents=2999` because Widget Pro cost 2999 when that order was placed. The product price later changed to 3499. This is how e-commerce is supposed to work:

- **`total_cents` on an order is a point-in-time snapshot** — it records what the customer actually agreed to pay. It's the financial contract.
- Retroactively rewriting order totals when catalog prices change would:
  - **Break accounting** — revenue reports, refund calculations, and reconciliation all depend on orders reflecting what was actually charged
  - **Misrepresent what customers paid** — order 42's customer paid $29.99, not $34.99
  - **Create legal/compliance risk** — financial records shouldn't be silently rewritten

The seed data confirms this: Widget Pro was created at 2999, orders were placed at that price, and the price was later bumped to 3499. Everything is consistent.
